ABOUT THE SUN

The Sun is the most important star of the system that gives it its name, and being so essential for life on our planet, it has historically been part of the symbolism of all cultures around the world. It is in good company: the Milky Way alone has more than 200,000 million stars, and the Sun is one of them. All the stars we can see with the naked eye are in the Milky Way.

WHY THAT NAME?

The word Sun has its origin in the Latin term sol, solis, which meant exactly the same thing: Sun, the luminous star, center of our planetary system.

Some related Latin words are solium, which means "throne, seat" -perhaps that is why the Romans called our star the chariot or throne of Apollo, and the Egyptians, the chariot of Amun-; solus, single, unique; and solidus, solid, consistent.
